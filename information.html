Rules

EpicVerse Server rules.

RULES
Clean Game
No profanity, spamming, excessive caps, English only in global chat (use /r or /msg for other languages)
No inappropriate skins, names, builds, signs, etc.
As with the 'keeping safe online' Do not engage in inappropriate behaviour on any platform. Please report it if you have concerns.
Pvp'ing without consent not allowed, in a pvp arenas.
Tp trap killing or death camping is not allowed.
No Hacked Clients
Any mod that gives you an advantage is not allowed
Hacks like; X-ray, Flying, Speed, etc are not allowed.
Do not use autoclickers in PvP
Do not abuse glitches
Duping, cheating, etc. Please report and you may even get a reward ;)
Nether Roof Building is strictly prohibited
Glitch onto the Nether Roof and building there is not allowed.
Server Griefing
No lava casts, encasing, flooding nor world, nether or end griefing
No trolling

Don't discuss punishments. Don't ban/mute evade with other accounts.
No inappropriate content
Referencing or talking about vulgar, sexual or drug topics etc.
Names, skins, banners, builds, item names, etc.
Do not impersonate staff
Name, colour, rank etc.
Do not leak personal information
Leaking personal information without the person's permission such as full names, addresses and any other personally identifiable information
No AFKing at areas which gives you an advantage
Auto-farms, grinders, etc.
No AFK devices
Pools etc
No Zero Tick/Flying machines
We may also ask you to remove any large areas which cause server lag
Please be efficient with your redstone machines

TOWNY AGE
Poaching of players from other towns, or towns from other nations is not allowed
Players and Towns must make the first approach!
Raiding of unclaimed bases/bankrupt towns is allowed, but not griefing them
All block changes are recorded by the server
Donating/giving away ranked perks or donator items is not allowed
Donators cannot give away donor perks and items, nor donor perks to players who are not the same rank.
Ranked perks in-game cannot be given used for other players that have not achieved the ranked perks.
Donors and ranked players may not give/sell/use perks and benefits for other players
This covers in-games ranks and donor ranks.
Tools, armour etc cannot be used/given to non-ranked players
There are strict limits on hoppers, chests, item frames, spawners etc.

Town System

Shows basic Towny commands.

/towny
? – Shows more Towny commands.
map – Shows the Towny map.
prices – Shows taxes/costs associated with running a town.
time – Shows time until next new-day (tax/upkeep collection.)
top residents (all/town/nation) – Shows top residents.
top land (all/resident/town) – Shows top land owners.

/plot
Shows the /plot commands.

? – Shows more plot commands.
claim – Resident command to claims a plot
claim auto – claim plots that are for sale
unclaim – Resident command to unclaim personally owned plots.
unclaim circle/rect – unclaim owned plots in a circle or rectangle shape.
unclaim (# (radius around current position)) – Radius of area to unclaim.
forsale/fs [amt] – Set a plot for sale.
forsale/fs circle/rect – Set a shape.
forsale/fs (# (radius around current position)) – Radius of area to set for sale.
notforsale/nfs – Set a plot to not be for sale.
set reset – Sets a shop/embassy/arena/wilds plot back to a normal plot.
set shop – Sets a plot to a shop plot.
set embassy – Sets a plot to an embassy plot.
set arena – Sets a plot to an arena plot.
set wilds – Sets a plot to a wilds plot.
set inn – Set a plot to an inn plot.
set name – allows a mayor or plot-owner to rename plots they own, overwriting the ~Unowned message.
perm – Shows the permission of the plot that the player is standing on
set perm (resident/ally/outsider) (on/off)
set perm (build/destroy/switch/itemuse) (on/off)
set perm (resident/ally/outsider) (build/destroy/switch/itemuse) (on/off)
set perm reset – Resets the plot in which you are standing on to the original perms

toggle
toggle fire – Turn on/off firespread in the plot in which you stand. Please note this will have no effect, as firespread is disabled serverwide.
toggle pvp – Turn on/off pvp in the plot in which you stand.
toggle explosion – Turn on/off explosions in the plot in which you stand.
toggle mob – Turn on/off hostile mobspawning in the plot in which you stand.

/resident
Shows a player their resident screen.

? – Shows /res commands available.
(resident) – Shows a player another player’s resident screen.
friend add (resident) – Resident adds online player to their friends list.
friend add+ (resident) – Resident adds offline player to their friends list.
friend remove (resident)– Resident removes online player from their friends list.
friend remove+ (resident)– Resident removes offline player from their friends list.
friend clearlist – Removes all friends from a resident’s friend list.
toggle map – Turns on map which refreshes when moving across plot borders.
toggle townclaim – Turns on mode where /town claim is automatically used when moving across plot borders.
toggle plotborder – Turns on smokey plot-border view. Border shows when players cross to different townblocks.
toggle reset – This turns off all modes that are active.
set perm (friend/ally/outsider) (on/off)
set perm (build/destroy/switch/itemuse) (on/off)
set perm (friend/ally/outsider) (build/destroy/switch/itemuse) (on/off)
set perm reset – This takes the perm line seen in the /resident screen and applies it to all plots personally owned by the player typing it.

/town
Shows a player their town’s town screen.

? – Shows /town commands available.
(town) – Shows a player another town’s town screen.
here – Shows you the town screen of the town in which you stand.
leave – Leaves a town.
list (page #) – Lists towns.
plots (townname) – Shows a helpful list of plots and their types/revenue which are owned by the town.
new (townname) – Creates new town.
delete - Deletes your town
add (resident) – Mayor command to add residents to your town.
kick (resident) – Mayor command to remove residents from your town.
spawn – Teleports you to your town’s spawn.
spawn (town) – Teleports you to another town’s spawn. This town will need to be in your nation.
claim – claims the townblock in which you are standing on
claim outpost – Claims an outpost for your town.
claim (# (radius around current position)) – Claims an area of townblocks around you
claim auto – Claims as many townblocks around you as is possible given Money in townbank and available townblocks.
unclaim – Mayor command to unclaim the townblock in which you stand.
unclaim all – Mayor command to unclaim all townblocks.
unclaim (# (radius around current position)) – Command to unclaim an area of townblocks around you.

Resource world

In EpicVerse, the resource world is typically used to address specific gameplay considerations and create a balanced and sustainable resource economy. Here are a few reasons why the resource world should be utilized:

Preservation of the main world: The server often aims to recreate the real-world geography and landmarks as accurately as possible. The resource world provides a separate space where players can gather resources without altering or depleting the main world's natural terrain, structures, or resources.
Sustainable resource gathering: By designating a resource world, server administrators can control and regenerate resources more effectively. It allows for the careful management of resource availability and distribution, ensuring that players have access to renewable resources and preventing the exhaustion or scarcity of valuable materials.
Economic balance: The resource world can be used to establish a player-driven economy. Certain resources may be intentionally limited or more abundant in the resource world, allowing for the creation of trade networks and economic interdependencies between players and communities. This dynamic can foster cooperation, competition, and specialization among players, enhancing the overall gameplay experience.
Resetting resources: Over time the main world might experience resource depletion due to extensive mining or other activities. By periodically resetting the resource world or regenerating its resources, the server can ensure a fresh supply of materials and prevent resource scarcity in the long run. This practice encourages ongoing exploration and resource gathering.
Overall, the resource world serves as a controlled environment to manage resources, maintain the integrity of the main world, promote a fair and sustainable resource economy, and enhance the multiplayer experience for all players.
